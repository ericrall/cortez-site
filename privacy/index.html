Privacy Policy

Cortez iOS App — by Eric Rall

What Cortez Does

Cortez turns your iPhone into a wireless keyboard, mouse, and AI assistant for any computer. It uses your phone's camera to see a computer screen, then sends keyboard and mouse commands via Bluetooth.

Data We Collect

We do not collect personal data. Specifically:

  • No accounts. Cortez does not require sign-up, login, or any account creation.
  • No analytics. We do not track usage, sessions, taps, or behavior.
  • No advertising. There are no ads and no ad tracking.
  • No data sold. We do not sell, share, or monetize any user data.

Camera

Cortez uses your iPhone's camera to view your computer screen. Camera frames are processed on-device for text recognition (OCR) and screen detection. When you give a command that requires AI analysis, a screenshot may be sent to our cloud service (hosted on Cloudflare Workers) for processing. Screenshots are not stored — they are processed in real-time and immediately discarded.

Microphone

If you use voice commands, Cortez accesses your microphone for speech recognition. Speech is processed using Apple's on-device speech recognition framework. Audio is not recorded, stored, or sent to any server.

Bluetooth

Cortez connects to your computer via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) as an HID (Human Interface Device) keyboard and mouse. This connection is direct between your iPhone and your computer — no data passes through our servers. The Bluetooth connection transmits only keyboard and mouse input reports.

Cloud Processing

When AI analysis is needed (complex commands that can't be handled locally), Cortez sends:

  • A screenshot of the computer screen (from the camera)
  • OCR text extracted from the screen
  • The user's command text

This data is sent to our Cloudflare Worker, which forwards it to AI model APIs (Anthropic Claude) for processing. No data is stored on our servers or by the AI providers beyond the immediate request. Cloudflare Workers are stateless — they process and respond without retaining data.

On-Device Storage

Cortez stores the following locally on your iPhone:

  • User preferences (display name, speed setting, platform choice)
  • Saved devices (Bluetooth device names and platform types)
  • Learned patterns (successful command sequences for faster replay)
  • Action logs (what commands were run and whether they succeeded, for self-improvement)

All local data is stored in your iPhone's app sandbox and synced via iCloud if enabled. You can delete all data by deleting the app.

Third-Party Services

  • Anthropic (Claude) — AI model for screen analysis and command generation. Anthropic Privacy Policy
  • Cloudflare Workers — Serverless compute for API routing. Cloudflare Privacy Policy
  • Apple CloudKit — Sync of on-device data (if iCloud is enabled). Apple Privacy Policy

Children

Cortez is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes

We may update this policy. Changes will be posted at this URL. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance.
